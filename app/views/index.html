What is the Soft Drinks Industry Levy?

The Soft Drink Industry Levy (SDIL) comes into effect from April 2018. It applies to those who package or import soft drinks with a sugar content of 5g or higher. These businesses will have to pay a charge for drinks that contain added sugar and the sugar content is 5g or more per 100 millilitres.

What is a liable drink?

A drink is liable if it:

  • has an alcohol content of less than 1.2%
  • is either ready to drink or, in order to be drunk must be diluted with water, mixed with crushed ice or processed to make crushed ice, mixed with carbon dioxide or a combination of these
  • contains added sugar such as mono-saccharides or di-saccharides
  • contains at least 5 grams of sugar per 100 millilitres
  • is not exempt

A drink is exempt if it:

  • has an alcohol content of more than 1.2%
  • is milk based or milk-substitute based
  • is an alcohol replacement drink
  • is used for medicinal or other specified purposes

Who needs to register?

You need to register if your business is involved in the production, packaging or importation of liable drinks, you’ll need to register.

You do not need to register if you:

  • do not produce liable drinks
  • produce less than 1 million litres of liable drinks per year

You can find out if you need to register if you’re not sure.
